How do you say you are running late professionally?

A good running late email should be quick, clear, and respectful. It should let the other person know you are delayed, give a realistic update when possible, and show that you respect their time.

This tool is best for a fast pre-arrival heads-up. It works well when you are still on your way and want to warn someone that you may arrive a little late.

When should you use a running late email?

Use a running late email when you want to send an early notice before you get there. It is useful for work, meetings, interviews, appointments, or other time-sensitive situations where a quick update helps manage expectations.

If the delay is already confirmed and you need a more accountable explanation, a late to work email may be the better fit. If you will not make it at all, use a call out of work or absent from work email instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write this type of email?
Start with a clear subject line, say that you are running late, include an updated arrival time if possible, and keep the message short and professional.

How is this different from a late to work email?
A running late email is usually a quick warning sent before arrival. A late to work email is more of a confirmed delay update or explanation once the lateness is already happening.

Can I edit the generated email?
Yes. You should personalize the draft so it matches your situation, relationship, and tone.
