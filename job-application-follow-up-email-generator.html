How do you follow up on a job application professionally?

A strong job follow up email should be polite, concise, and specific. The best messages reference the original application or outreach, express continued interest, and make it easy for the recipient to respond.

This tool combines multiple job follow up scenarios into one place, including checking application status, following up after sending a resume, emailing a recruiter, and reaching out after no response.

When should you send a follow up email?

In most cases, waiting about one to two weeks before following up is appropriate. This gives the employer or recruiter time to review applications while still keeping you visible.

If you are following up after a conversation or resume submission, shorter timing may make sense depending on the situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a job follow up email include?
It should reference the original application or message, express continued interest, and remain professional and concise.

How do I follow up without sounding annoying?
Keep the message short, respectful, and spaced out appropriately. Avoid sending multiple messages too quickly.

Should I follow up more than once?
In most cases, one or two follow ups are appropriate depending on the situation.
