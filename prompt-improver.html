Good prompts are specific

A better prompt gives the AI context, goal, audience, tone, constraints, and output format. Specific inputs usually produce better outputs.

Tell the AI what to avoid

Negative instructions can be useful. Saying what not to include helps avoid generic filler, wrong tone, awkward formatting, or unwanted structure.

Format matters

If you want bullets, steps, examples, a table, a draft, or a finished answer, say so directly. The clearer the format, the easier the result is to use.

Start simple, then refine

You do not need a perfect prompt on the first try. A strong prompt gives the model enough direction to produce something useful immediately.
