Pause before replying

Difficult texts can trigger a fast emotional reaction. A better response usually starts with slowing down and deciding what the message actually needs.

Respond to the point

Good responses do not answer every emotional detail. They address the main issue clearly without getting pulled into a long defensive spiral.

Clear is kinder

Vague replies can create more tension. A calm, direct message often feels more respectful than a long explanation that leaves the other person guessing.

Text has limits

Some conversations should not happen entirely by text. If the topic is too serious, a short response that suggests talking later may be better.
