What makes a good dating bio

A good bio feels specific, honest, and easy to respond to. It should give someone a real sense of your personality without trying too hard.

Avoid generic profile clichés

Lines like “good vibes only,” “partner in crime,” and “fluent in sarcasm” usually blend in. Specific details make you more memorable.

Show personality through specifics

Mention real interests, routines, values, and quirks. The goal is not to impress everyone; it is to sound like a real person someone could talk to.

Match the app and goal

A Hinge bio can be more thoughtful, Bumble can be more warm and confident, and Tinder may work better when it is short and punchy.
