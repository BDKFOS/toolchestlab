How do you write a sick day email professionally?

A good sick day email should be brief, clear, and work-focused. The strongest messages explain that you are sick, say whether you will be out for the day or unavailable for work, and avoid adding unnecessary medical detail.

This tool is specifically for illness-related absences. If you are missing work for another reason such as an emergency, transportation problem, or general same-day issue, a different absence email tool may fit better.

When should you use a sick day email?

Use a sick day email when you need to notify work that you are out because you are ill, not feeling well, or unable to safely or effectively work. It helps you communicate quickly while keeping the tone professional and appropriate.

Most sick day emails only need a few essentials: that you are sick, whether you will be offline or unavailable, and when you expect to follow up with an update.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write this type of email?
Start with a clear subject line, state that you are sick, explain that you will be out or unavailable, and keep the message short and professional.

How is this different from a call out of work email?
A sick day email is specifically for illness. A call out of work email is broader and can also cover emergencies, car trouble, childcare issues, or other last-minute reasons.

Can I edit the generated email?
Yes. You should personalize the draft so it matches your situation, workplace, and tone.
