How do you tell your boss you will be late to work?

A good late to work email should be short, clear, and respectful. It should say that you are delayed, explain the situation briefly, and include a realistic arrival update when possible.

This tool is best when you already know you are going to be late and need to explain the delay professionally. It is more about acknowledging and updating than giving a casual heads-up.

When should you use a late to work email?

Use a late to work email when you are already delayed or have confirmed that you will arrive after your scheduled start time. It works well for traffic, transportation problems, childcare issues, or other same-day delays.

If you are only sending a quick heads-up before you are officially late, a running late email may be the better fit. If you will miss work entirely, a call out of work or absent from work email is usually more appropriate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write this type of email?
Start with a clear subject line, explain briefly why you are late, include your updated arrival time if possible, and keep the tone professional and accountable.

How is this different from a running late email?
A late to work email is for when the delay is already happening or confirmed. A running late email is more of an early heads-up before arrival.

Can I edit the generated email?
Yes. You should personalize the draft so it matches your situation, relationship, and tone.
