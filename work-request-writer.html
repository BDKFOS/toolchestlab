Make the ask obvious

A good work request should make it easy for the recipient to understand what you need, why you need it, and what decision or action comes next.

Give useful context

Include enough background to make the request reasonable, but avoid burying the ask in a long explanation.

Stay confident and polite

You do not need to sound nervous or overly apologetic. Clear, respectful wording usually works better than over-explaining.

Include timing when needed

If a decision has a deadline, include it clearly. This helps managers, coworkers, and clients respond faster.
