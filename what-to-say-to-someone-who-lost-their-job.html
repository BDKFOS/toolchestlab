What do you say to someone who lost their job?

A good message usually acknowledges the difficulty of the situation and offers support without rushing them past it. It helps to sound encouraging, but not overly positive or dismissive.

This generator helps you find natural wording for layoffs, sudden job loss, difficult work transitions, and supportive check-ins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I try to cheer them up right away?
Usually it helps to acknowledge the loss first. Encouragement lands better when it does not skip over what happened.

Can I offer help too?
Yes. Practical offers of support can be helpful if they feel genuine and specific.
