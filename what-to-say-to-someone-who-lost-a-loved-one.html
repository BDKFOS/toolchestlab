What do you say to someone who lost a loved one?

A good message usually acknowledges the loss, expresses care, and keeps the focus on the person who is hurting. You do not have to make the pain better. You just want to sound present, respectful, and human.

This generator helps you find natural condolence wording for situations involving the loss of a parent, spouse, sibling, child, friend, or another loved one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this in a sympathy card?
Yes. Many of the generated responses can work in a sympathy card, text, message, or spoken conversation.

Should I mention the person who died?
Often yes, if it feels appropriate. Mentioning the person can make the message feel more real and personal.
