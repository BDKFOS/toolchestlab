How do you set boundaries with words?

The strongest boundary-setting usually sounds calm and clear. It helps to say what you need without overexplaining, over-apologizing, or turning it into an argument.

This generator helps you find natural wording when you need to set a limit, protect your time or energy, or say no more clearly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should boundary-setting be firm?
Usually yes, but it does not need to be harsh. Calm and clear is often strongest.

Do I have to explain myself a lot?
Usually no. Overexplaining can weaken the boundary instead of strengthening it.
