How do you send a second follow up email without sounding pushy?

A second follow up email should acknowledge that you already reached out, keep the tone respectful, and make it easy for the recipient to respond. It should be short, polite, and professional.

People often send second follow ups after no response from clients, recruiters, hiring managers, leads, or business contacts. The goal is to stay visible without sounding impatient.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it okay to send a second follow up email?
Yes. In many professional situations, a polite second follow up is completely normal if enough time has passed.

How long should I wait before sending a second follow up?
It depends on the situation, but many people wait several business days after the first follow up.

How do I avoid sounding pushy?
Keep the message short, professional, and easy to respond to. Avoid sounding frustrated or demanding.
