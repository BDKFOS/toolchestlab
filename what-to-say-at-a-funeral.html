What do you say at a funeral?

At a funeral, the best words are usually brief, respectful, and sincere. You do not need a perfect speech. A simple expression of sympathy and care is often enough.

This generator helps you find natural wording for funeral conversations, condolences, and hard moments when you want to be kind without overdoing it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it okay to keep it very short?
Yes. Short and sincere often feels more appropriate than saying too much.

Should I mention the person who died?
If it feels natural, yes. A gentle mention can make your words feel more personal and meaningful.
