How do you write a salary negotiation email professionally?

A good salary negotiation email should be respectful, clear, and confident. The strongest messages show appreciation for the opportunity while making a specific, reasonable compensation request based on experience, market value, or the scope of the role.

This salary negotiation email generator helps you draft professional emails for job offers, raises, internal role changes, and compensation discussions without sounding awkward or overly aggressive.

Simply describe the situation, choose the tone and recipient, and the tool will generate a salary negotiation email you can send immediately or edit to better match your style.

When should you send a salary negotiation email?

A salary negotiation email is most useful after receiving an offer, during a compensation review, or when your responsibilities have clearly increased and you are asking for pay to reflect that change.

In most cases, the best salary negotiation emails are calm, specific, and easy for the other person to respond to. A clear request with a professional tone is usually more effective than a long explanation.
