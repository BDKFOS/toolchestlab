How do you write a professional apology email?

A strong professional apology email is clear, accountable, and focused on resolution. Acknowledge what happened, take responsibility, and explain what you are doing next.

Common workplace situations include delays, missed deadlines, late replies, not responding to messages, confusion caused by unclear information, recipient mistakes, wrong emails, wrong attachments, missing attachments, inconvenience caused by disruptions, workplace oversights, apologizing to a manager, client-facing service issues, customer service issues, missed meetings, short notice changes, and more formal written work apologies. The goal is to maintain trust while moving the situation forward.

Keep your message respectful and concise, avoid over-explaining, and focus on clarity and next steps.
