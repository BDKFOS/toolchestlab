General Workplace Apology

Subject: Apology Hi [Name], I wanted to apologize for [issue]. I understand this may have caused inconvenience, and I take responsibility for it. I am working to address the situation and will follow up with [next step]. Thank you for your patience. Best, [Your Name]

Apology to a Manager

Subject: Apology for the Oversight Hi [Name], I want to apologize for [issue]. I understand the impact this may have had, and I take responsibility for the mistake. I am already taking steps to correct it and will keep you updated on progress. Best, [Your Name]

Client Apology

Subject: Apology Hi [Name], I wanted to apologize for [issue]. I understand this may have affected your experience, and I appreciate your patience. We are addressing it now and I will follow up with an update by [date/time]. Best, [Your Name]

Short & Professional

Subject: Apology Hi [Name], I’m sorry for [issue]. I take responsibility and appreciate your patience. I’ll follow up with [next step] shortly. Best, [Your Name]

How do you write a professional apology email?

A professional apology email should be clear, respectful, and accountable. It should explain the issue briefly, acknowledge responsibility, and, when appropriate, include a corrective step or next action.

This page is for broader workplace and business apologies. It works well when you need to apologize to a client, manager, boss, coworker, employee, or other professional contact while keeping the tone polished and credible.

The best professional apology emails sound human without becoming too casual. Clear communication helps protect trust, relationships, and professional credibility.

When should you use this tool?

Use this tool when the apology needs to sound business-appropriate and work-focused. It is best for missed meetings, workplace mistakes, service issues, miscommunication, and professional situations where tone matters.

If your issue is specifically about a delayed reply, missed timeline, or client-facing delay, one of the delay-specific apology tools may fit better.
