How do you write a professional apology email?

A professional apology email should be clear, respectful, and accountable. It should explain the issue briefly, acknowledge responsibility, and, when appropriate, include a corrective step or next action.

This tool is specifically for workplace and business apologies. It works well when you need to apologize to a client, manager, boss, coworker, employee, or other professional contact while maintaining a polished and credible tone.

The best professional apology emails sound human without becoming too casual. Clear communication helps protect trust, relationships, and your professional reputation.

When should you use this tool?

Use this tool when the apology needs to sound business-appropriate and work-focused. It is best for missed meetings, delayed replies, workplace mistakes, service issues, miscommunication, or other professional situations where tone matters.

If you need a broader apology tool for personal, school, or mixed-use situations, the AI Apology Email Generator is a better fit. If you need a more formal documented workplace apology, an apology letter for work may fit better.
