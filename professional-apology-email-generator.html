How do you write a professional apology email?

A strong professional apology email is clear, accountable, and focused on resolution. Acknowledge what happened, take responsibility, and explain what you are doing next.

Common workplace situations include delays, missed deadlines, late replies, miscommunication, and service issues. The goal is to maintain trust while moving the situation forward.

Keep your message respectful and concise; avoid over-explaining, and focus on clarity and next steps.

When should you use this tool?

Use this when you need a professional, workplace-appropriate apology. It works for clients, managers, coworkers, and business communication where tone and clarity matter.
